King David the Builder

Introduction

King David IV of Georgia, also known as David the Builder, was one of the most important figures in Georgian history. His reign in the 12th century marked a golden age for Georgia, with territorial expansion, cultural flourishing, and the establishment of a powerful kingdom.

Key Achievements of King David

  • Unified Georgia and expanded its territories through military conquest
  • Defeated the Seljuk Turks, restoring Georgian dominance in the Caucasus region
  • Strengthened the Georgian Orthodox Church and fostered cultural growth
  • Built strong fortifications and established a central authority in Georgia

Important Events During David the Builder's Reign

  • 1089: David IV ascended the throne of Georgia
  • 1121: Led the Georgian forces to victory in the Battle of Didgori, defeating the Seljuk Turks
  • 1122: Captured Tbilisi, the capital of Georgia, restoring it to Georgian control
  • 1130: Expanded Georgian influence into neighboring regions, making Georgia a regional power
