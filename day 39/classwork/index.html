Georgian Wehrmacht Legion

Overview

The Georgian Wehrmacht Legion was a military unit of Georgian volunteers that fought on the side of Nazi Germany during World War II. It was formed primarily of ethnic Georgians and others from the Caucasus region, driven by various motivations, including anti-Soviet sentiments and nationalist aspirations.

History

  • Formed in 1942, as a part of the collaboration between Nazi Germany and anti-Soviet Georgian nationalists.
  • Initially intended to fight against the Soviet Union, with the hopes of achieving Georgian independence.
  • Served alongside the German Army in various campaigns, particularly on the Eastern Front.
  • The legion was absorbed into the Wehrmacht and participated in multiple battles, including the defense of German-occupied territories in the Soviet Union.
  • Disbanded after Germany's defeat in 1945, with many members either captured by the Soviets or fleeing to the West.

  • In addition to fighting, the legion was involved in various intelligence-gathering and propaganda operations.
